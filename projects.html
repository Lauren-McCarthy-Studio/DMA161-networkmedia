p1: Putting Text on the Web

Create a web page for a piece of writing. This writing could be yours, it could be written for this purpose, it could be written by you in the past. It could be someone else’s—found in a book, a zine, a manifesto, or elsewhere online. Make sure the text interests you and speaks to you, as we'll be working with it and designing based off of it over the first few classes. I'd recommend something long form, but not unwieldy.

We’ll learn the fundamentals of HTML and CSS through this exercise. First formatting it with basic HTML tags then we’ll move on to inline styles, then eventually reformatting it with a cascading style sheet.

Throughout the assignment, we’ll draw on past skills with graphic design and typography and apply them to this new medium. However, this assignment is not focused on formalism or best practices. Experiment, try new things, take risks, and be expressive. The goal of this assignment is to experiment, we’ll use the knowledge gained more formally in later projects.

Things we’ll consider:

  • What does it mean to publish text online (versus in print)?
  • How does your page contextualize / recontextualize the writing?
  • How can other elements enhance, change, or interfere with the text?

p1p1: Find and Transcribe Text — DUE 4/10

Find a piece of writing that speaks to you and transcribe it in HTML using tags: headings, paragraphs, bold, italic, strong, emphasis, line breaks, lists, ordered lists etc. Upload this text as an HTML file to your DMA server space, and link to it from your Under Construction page.
